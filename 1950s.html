Welcome to the 1950s!

Post-War Suburban Growth

During the 1950s, many families began to move into suburban areas, seeking a more comfortable and spacious lifestyle. The post-war economic boom allowed for increased home ownership and the development of new housing developments. This period saw the rise of consumer culture, with families purchasing new appliances and automobiles to enhance their daily lives.

Rise of Modern Living

Furniture became more affordable and accessible, leading to a surge in demand for stylish and functional pieces. The 1950s marked the beginning of a new era in home design, with an emphasis on modernity and convenience. This decade laid the foundation for the growth of companies like IKEA, which would later revolutionize the furniture industry with its innovative designs and affordable prices.

The Birth of IKEA

This led to Invar Kamprad, a 17 year old entrepreneur, to create the (arguably) most succesful brand to this day. The IKEA Nytt catalogue released in 1948, and would become the basis for every IKEA catalogue that came after it. In the begining, IKEA stuck to selling briefcases and watches, eventually dipping into the furniture market as demand grew.
